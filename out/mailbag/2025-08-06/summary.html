MLB Mailbag: Schwarber, Braves, Story, Naylor, Tucker, Rockies

MLB Trade Rumors Daily Digest

Key Insights

  • Kyle Schwarber is set to hit free agency this offseason after a career-best season in Philadelphia. The Phillies may look to extend him on a 4-year, $125M contract ($31.25M AAV) to keep him from reaching the open market.
  • Schwarber has already reached 4 WAR this season, a new career-high, and is on pace for over 5 WAR. His previous best was 3.3 WAR in 2021.
  • Only two DHs age 33+ have gotten 4-year deals in free agency (Nelson Cruz, Victor Martinez), providing a potential precedent for Schwarber's next contract.
  • The Phillies have payroll flexibility with several key players coming off the books, potentially allowing them to give Schwarber a $30M AAV extension to avoid him hitting free agency.
  • However, there are questions whether a 4-year, $125M deal for the 33-year-old Schwarber would be a wise long-term investment for the Phillies, given his defensive limitations.
  • The 2026-27 free agent class is projected to be relatively weak, potentially increasing demand and contract values for impact bats like Schwarber.
